1. Your Role as District Captain

As a District Captain, you are the community liaison for your assigned HIBCW district. You serve as the first point of contact between local businesses, residents, and the broader HIBCW network. Your role is built around five core responsibilities:

  • Monitoring incidents reported within your district
  • Reviewing and acting on membership applications for your area
  • Keeping your district's members informed through timely alerts
  • Maintaining accurate member and captain records for your area
  • Coordinating with HIBCW admins when escalation is needed
👮 Your Access is Scoped to Your District

When you sign in, the admin panel automatically filters everything to your district. You will only see incidents, members, and applications that belong to your assigned area. This is by design — it keeps things focused and ensures member privacy across districts.

2. Signing In to the Admin Panel

There are two versions of the admin panel — a full desktop version and a mobile-optimized version. Both use the same login credentials.

Desktop Admin
  • Go to your Vercel deployment URL and add "/admin.html" (e.g., hibcw.vercel.app/admin.html)
  • Enter your email address and password
  • Click "Sign In →"
Mobile Admin
  • Visit hibcw.vercel.app/admin-mobile.html on your phone or tablet
  • Alternatively, visit admin.html on a mobile device — a prompt will appear offering to redirect you
  • Same credentials as the desktop version
🔑 Forgot Your Password?

On the sign-in screen, tap or click "Forgot password?" and enter your email address. A reset link will be sent to your inbox. If you do not receive it within a few minutes, check your spam folder or contact your HIBCW administrator.

After Signing In
  • Identifies you as a Captain and scopes all data to your district
  • Takes you directly to the Incidents screen (desktop: Districts view)
  • Displays your name and district in the header or dashboard area
  • Hides buttons and features that are reserved for admins
3. Your Dashboard

When you navigate to the Districts screen, your personal captain dashboard is displayed at the top before the district cards. This gives you a quick snapshot of your district at a glance.

3.1 Stats Tiles
  • Active — number of currently active (unarchived) incidents in your district
  • Month — incidents reported this calendar month
  • Week — incidents reported in the current week
  • Members — total verified HIBCW members in your district
3.2 Recent Incidents

A quick list of the 8 most recent incidents in your district, color-coded by severity. Click or tap any row to open the full incident detail view. Archived incidents are noted inline.

3.3 Alert Composer & Log

The dashboard includes a text area to compose and send a district alert, as well as a log of your most recent alerts. See Section 4.4 for full instructions on sending alerts.

4. What You CAN Do — Full Instructions
4.1 Viewing & Searching Incidents

The Incidents screen shows all active incidents in your district. You can search and filter to find specific reports.

On Desktop (admin.html)
  1. Click "Districts" in the left sidebar or navigate to the Incidents tab
  2. Your district's incidents are automatically pre-filtered
  3. Use the search bar to search by type, address, or description
  4. Use the Type and Status dropdowns to narrow results
  5. Click "View" on any row to open the full incident detail panel
On Mobile (admin-mobile.html)
  1. The Incidents screen is the default landing screen after login
  2. Use the search bar at the top to filter by keyword
  3. Use the Type and Status dropdowns to narrow results
  4. Tap any incident card to open the detail sheet
💡 Tip

The Status dropdown defaults to "Active." Switch it to "Archived" to review resolved incidents, or choose "All" to see everything at once.

4.2 Archiving & Restoring Incidents

You can archive incidents in your district when they have been resolved, are duplicates, or are no longer relevant. Archived incidents are removed from the public-facing map but are not deleted.

To Archive an Incident
1
Open the Incident
  • Find the incident in the list or dashboard
  • Click or tap "View" to open the detail panel
2
Archive It
  • Click or tap the "📦 Archive" button in the action row
  • Confirm when prompted
  • The incident will be removed from the active list and the public map
To Restore an Archived Incident
  1. Change the Status filter to "Archived"
  2. Find the incident and open it
  3. Click or tap "↩ Restore"
  4. The incident returns to active status and reappears on the public map
⚠️ Important

Archiving is reversible. Deleting is permanent and is reserved for admins only. If you believe an incident should be permanently deleted, contact your HIBCW administrator.

4.3 Adding Follow-Up Notes

Notes allow you to log follow-up information on an incident — such as police contact, community feedback, or status updates — without altering the original report.

1
Open the Incident Detail
  • Find and open any incident in your district
  • Scroll to the bottom of the detail panel to find the "📝 Follow-Up Notes" section
2
Write Your Note
  • Type your note in the text field at the bottom
  • Click "Add" or press Enter to save it
  • Your note will appear in the log with your name and timestamp

Notes are visible to all admin and captain users. They are not shown on the public-facing site.

4.4 Sending District Alerts

District alerts allow you to communicate directly with your community. Once sent, alerts appear on the public-facing site — on the map page and in the "📢 Alerts" section of the mobile app. Alerts are visible to everyone who visits the site.

Step 1 — Choose an Alert Type

Before typing your message, select the alert type that best describes the situation. The type controls the color, icon, and urgency shown to the public.

TypeUse When
⛔ EMERGENCYActive threat or imminent danger — triggers 911 call buttons publicly
👁 BOLOBe on the lookout — describe the suspect, vehicle, and last known location
🟡 AMBER / MISSINGMissing child or vulnerable adult
🚨 INCIDENT ALERTCrime just occurred or in progress
🔁 PATTERN ALERTSame suspect or MO has hit multiple businesses
💳 SCAM ALERTFraud, counterfeit, or con artist activity
🚫 TRESPASS ALERTKnown trespasser — alert neighboring businesses
🫶 WELFARE CHECKConcerning person, no clear crime yet
✅ ALL CLEARSituation resolved — follow-up to a prior alert
📋 INFO / ADVISORYGeneral awareness, no immediate threat
💡 Safety Reminders Are Automatic

You do not need to add emergency numbers to your message. Critical alerts (EMERGENCY, BOLO, AMBER) automatically display "Call 911" and "HPD Non-Emergency (808) 935-2100" tap-to-call buttons to the public. All other alert types show a quieter reminder line automatically.

From the Desktop Dashboard
  1. Navigate to the Districts screen (left sidebar)
  2. Your dashboard appears at the top with the "📢 Send District Alert" box
  3. Select the Alert Type from the dropdown
  4. Type your message in the text area
  5. Optionally attach a photo or video using the 📎 Attach button
  6. Click "📤 Send Alert"
From the Mobile Dashboard
  1. Tap the "📍 Districts" tab in the bottom navigation
  2. Your dashboard is shown at the top of the screen
  3. Select the Alert Type from the picker
  4. Type your message in the alert composer box
  5. Tap "📤 Send"
✍️ Writing Effective Alerts

Keep alerts concise and specific. Include: what happened or is happening, which area is affected, what members should do or watch for, and any relevant dates or times. For BOLO alerts, always include a physical description and direction of travel if known.

🌐 Public Visibility

Alerts appear on the public site within seconds of being sent. They are displayed with your name and the date/time. Do not include sensitive personal information, names of individuals, or unconfirmed details in an alert.

🚫 No Advertising Policy

Alerts may only be used to communicate safety-relevant community information. You may not use alerts to promote businesses, products, services, events, or any commercial interest — even businesses you personally support or have a relationship with. This policy exists to protect the integrity and community trust of HIBCW. Violations may result in loss of platform access. See Section 15 of the HIBCW Terms of Use.

4.5 Archiving or Cancelling an Alert

You have two ways to remove an alert from the public feed:

  • 📦 Archive — removes the alert from the public feed immediately but keeps it permanently as a record. This is the preferred option. Archived alerts can be viewed, restored, or permanently deleted later. Use archiving to preserve a history of district activity for reporting, law enforcement briefings, and proof of concept purposes.
  • ✕ Delete — permanently removes the alert and any attached media. This cannot be undone. Only use this if the alert was sent in error and should not be kept as a record.
To Archive an Alert
  1. Open the Districts screen and scroll to your dashboard
  2. Find the alert in the "📋 Recent Alerts" log
  3. Click or tap the "📦 Archive" button
  4. Confirm when prompted — the alert is immediately removed from the public site but retained as a record
To View Archived Alerts
  1. Scroll down to the "📦 Archived Alerts" section on your dashboard
  2. Click "Load" — all archived alerts for your district will appear
  3. Each record shows the full message, who sent it, when it was sent, who archived it, and when
  4. Use "↩ Restore" to put an alert back on the public feed, or "✕ Delete Permanently" to remove it forever
📋 Best Practice

Send an ✅ ALL CLEAR alert when a situation is resolved rather than immediately archiving the original alert. This keeps the community informed that the situation has been handled, then archive both alerts afterward to preserve the complete record.

4.6 Viewing & Searching Members

You can view all verified HIBCW members in your district.

On Desktop
  1. Click "Members" in the left sidebar — results are automatically filtered to your district
  2. Use the search bar to find members by name, email, business, or phone
  3. Member rows show name, email, business name, phone, and assigned captain
On Mobile
  1. Tap the "👥 Members" tab in the bottom navigation
  2. Use the search bar and district filter at the top to find members
  3. Tap any member card to see their full contact details

Member management (adding, editing, removing members) is reserved for admins. Contact your HIBCW administrator if a member needs to be updated or removed.

4.7 Managing Applications

You can view, approve, and reject membership applications submitted by businesses in your district. This gives you direct authority over who joins the HIBCW network in your area.

  • Applications are automatically filtered to your district
  • You can see pending, approved, and rejected applications
  • You can approve pending applicants, reject them, revoke approved members, or re-approve previously rejected ones
  • Deleting an application permanently is reserved for admins
To Approve an Application
1
Find the Application
  • Navigate to the Applications screen (desktop: left sidebar → Applications; mobile: 📋 Apply tab)
  • The filter defaults to "Pending" — pending applications for your district appear here
2
Review the Details
  • Check the applicant's name, business name, address, email, and phone
  • If they included a note, it appears below the contact details
  • Verify this is a legitimate business in your district
3
Approve
  • Click or tap "✅ Approve" and confirm
  • The applicant is added to the verified members list
  • Their report submissions will show a "✅ Verified Member" badge on the public map
To Reject an Application
  1. Find the pending application in your district
  2. Click or tap "❌ Reject" and confirm — the application status changes to Rejected
To Revoke an Approved Member
  1. Change the status filter to "Approved"
  2. Find the member and click "Revoke"
  3. Their verified status is removed and they are removed from the member list
⚠️ Delete is Admin-Only

You can approve, reject, revoke, and re-approve applications in your district. Permanently deleting an application record is reserved for HIBCW administrators.

4.8 Viewing the Districts & Directory

The Districts screen gives you a full picture of all nine HIBCW districts, their assigned captains, and contact information.

Overview Tab
  • Shows a card for each district with the captain's photo, name, business, and contact information
  • Displays district stats: member count, incidents this month/week/today, and last reported date
  • Shows a type breakdown bar chart for active incidents in each district
  • Includes a "👥 View Members" button that opens the member roster for any district
Directory Tab
  • A searchable list view showing: district, captain name, business, email, and phone for all 9 districts
  • Tap the 🖨 print button to generate a printable PDF directory of all captains
Member Roster (from district card)
  • Tap "👥 View Members" on any district card to open the roster for that district
  • Includes a search bar and a "🖨 Print Roster" button to export a formatted PDF
5. What You CANNOT Do

The following actions are reserved for HIBCW admins and superadmins. This ensures data integrity, member privacy, and consistent management of the platform across all districts.

  • Permanently delete incidents (archiving is the captain equivalent)
  • Add or remove verified member emails from the system
  • Edit member profile details (name, address, business info, district assignment)
  • Permanently delete applications
  • Export incident or member data as CSV
  • View incidents, members, or applications from other districts
  • Send district alerts to districts other than your own
  • Access the Manage Admins screen (superadmin only)
  • Invite or remove other captains or admin accounts
  • Upload or change the captain photo for other districts
📞 Need an Admin Action?

If you need something done that is outside your permissions — such as removing a member, correcting a profile, or permanently deleting a record — contact your HIBCW administrator. They can act quickly on your behalf.

8. Tips & Best Practices
Staying on Top of Your District
  • Check the admin panel at least once a day during active periods — new incidents appear in real time
  • Review the "Recent Incidents" section on your dashboard each morning for an overnight summary
  • Use the "This Week" stat tile to gauge whether activity is trending up or down in your district
Archiving Responsibly
  • Archive an incident when it has been resolved, confirmed as a duplicate, or is no longer relevant to active community awareness
  • Add a follow-up note before archiving to document why — e.g., "Resolved, HPD confirmed report filed"
  • Do not archive incidents solely because they are old — recurring patterns are valuable data
Sending Effective Alerts
  • Always select the correct alert type — this sets the color, icon, and urgency shown to the public. Critical types (EMERGENCY, BOLO, AMBER) automatically display 911 and HPD non-emergency call buttons to viewers
  • Only send alerts for significant, actionable information — avoid over-alerting, which can cause members to tune out
  • Be specific: include location, description, type of incident, and what to watch for. For BOLO alerts, always include physical description and last known direction of travel
  • Archive rather than delete — archived alerts are kept permanently as a record. Use this to preserve proof of activity for reporting, law enforcement briefings, and demonstrating HIBCW's value
  • Send an ✅ ALL CLEAR when a situation is resolved before archiving — this keeps the community informed that the threat has passed
  • If you're unsure whether something warrants an alert, consult your HIBCW administrator first
  • Never use alerts for advertising or commercial promotion — this includes promoting businesses, events, products, or services of any kind, even ones you personally support. Alerts are strictly for community safety information. See the HIBCW Terms of Use, Section 15.
Keeping Your Profile Current
  • Make sure your captain photo is up to date — it appears on the district directory and builds community trust
  • If your phone number or email changes, contact your HIBCW admin to update your profile
