Your Launch Playbook

HIBCW is Hawaii Island's free, community-led business crime watch network. This strategy takes it from internal KIAA adoption to island-wide recognition across every district and platform — no Facebook required.

Platform Strategy: Instagram, Nextdoor, LinkedIn, and YouTube. Each platform reaches a distinct audience segment on Hawaii Island — Instagram for visual community reach, Nextdoor for hyperlocal neighborhood trust, LinkedIn for business owners and chamber professionals, YouTube for longer-form explainer content and search discoverability.
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

KIAA Soft Launch

Introduce HIBCW to current KIAA members through a private email drip, a direct outreach letter, and an in-person (or virtual) demo session. Goal: onboard your founding member base before public launch. Create social proof before going wide.

Phase 2 · Weeks 5–12

Island-Wide Campaign

Roll out district by district across Instagram, Nextdoor, LinkedIn, and YouTube. Coordinate with local chambers of commerce, neighborhood groups, and TPD community liaisons. Amplify real member stories as they emerge.

Platform Roles

Why These 4 Platforms

Instagram

Visual Awareness

Reels and Story posts reach small business owners, shop managers, and community-minded locals. District-specific posts with map visuals drive clicks to hibcw.vercel.app.

Nextdoor

Hyperlocal Trust

Nextdoor is the #1 neighborhood platform in Hawaii. Each of the 9 districts maps to Nextdoor neighborhoods. Announcements here feel like a neighbor talking to a neighbor — ideal for community safety programs.

LinkedIn

Business Owner Reach

Hawaii Island chamber members, business owners, and managers live on LinkedIn. KIAA's organizational credibility translates directly here. Posts announcing the tool get shared within professional networks.

YouTube

Search-Discoverable Explainers

A short 2–3 minute walkthrough video ("How to Report an Incident on HIBCW") will rank for local searches and can be embedded on future KIAA website pages. YouTube Shorts repurposed for Instagram Reels maximize production value.

Also Recommended

Email (KIAA List)

Your existing KIAA email list is the highest-trust, highest-conversion channel you have. Three targeted emails over 4 weeks (announcement, demo invite, follow-up) will drive the majority of early sign-ups before social even warms up.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

KIAA Soft Launch

Before going public, bring your existing KIAA member network onboard. They become your founding members, social proof, and word-of-mouth engine.

Why soft launch first? HIBCW's value compounds with network size. If community members visit the map on Day 1 and see zero incidents, they'll bounce. By seeding your KIAA members first, you build an active data layer before public launch — so when Island-wide promotion begins, the map is alive and credible.
Week 1
Member Announcement Email
Send a personal email from KIAA leadership to your full member list. Subject line: "KIAA just launched something new for Hawaii Island businesses." Introduce HIBCW, the URL, and invite them to explore the map. Keep it warm, direct, and brief — this is a teaser, not a manual.

Week 2
Intro Letter + Member Packet Distribution
Send the official HIBCW intro letter (template-intro-letter.pdf) via mail or hand-delivery to every KIAA member business. Include the district-specific flyer for their area. Captains should begin outreach to their district businesses directly. This is the formal onboarding touchpoint — tactile, professional, and stays on desks.

Virtual or In-Person Demo Session

Record the session for a YouTube explainer video. Invite questions and collect testimonials from early adopters. Aim for 15–30 attendees.

Post the first teaser on Instagram and LinkedIn — a simple shield graphic with the copy "Something new is coming to Hawaii Island. Built by KIAA. Built for business." No URL yet — build anticipation. Begin scheduling district-by-district Nextdoor posts for Phase 2.

Phase 1 Success Gate

Before Moving to Phase 2

Confirm at least 20 KIAA member businesses are registered and the map has active incident data from at least 3 districts. This ensures your public launch lands on a living, credible platform — not an empty map.

Phase 2 · Weeks 5–12

Island-Wide Campaign

With your founding members onboard and the map active, open the floodgates. A district-by-district rollout ensures each community feels personally addressed, not blasted with a generic island-wide ad.

Week 5
Public Launch Announcement
Coordinate a simultaneous announcement across Instagram, LinkedIn, and Nextdoor (all districts at once). Post the YouTube explainer video. Issue a press release to West Hawaii Today and Hawaii Tribune-Herald covering HIBCW as a new KIAA community safety initiative. Email your full list with "HIBCW is now open to all Hawaii Island businesses — join free today."
Instagram LinkedIn Nextdoor YouTube Live Press Release
Weeks 6–7
District Spotlight Series (Instagram + Nextdoor)
One district per post, rotating through all 9 over two weeks. Each Instagram carousel or Reel shows that district's map activity, names the district captain (with permission), and includes a call-to-action to "join your district." Simultaneously post on each district's Nextdoor neighborhood. This personalizes the campaign at the community level.
Instagram Reels Nextdoor District-Specific
Week 8
Member Story Feature
Reach out to 2–3 early KIAA members who can speak to the value of HIBCW. A short quote or 30-second video testimonial from a real Hilo or Kona business owner is worth ten polished ads. Feature on Instagram Stories, LinkedIn, and Nextdoor. Caption: "A business like yours, protected by their community."
Testimonials Instagram Stories LinkedIn
Weeks 9–10
Chamber & TPD Partnership Posts
Coordinate co-branded posts with the Kona-Kohala Chamber, East Hawaii Regional Chamber, and any TPD community liaison officers who support the program. Partner posts get organic reach into networks you don't currently own. A brief quote from a law enforcement contact lends immediate credibility to the platform.
Chamber Partners TPD Endorsement LinkedIn
Weeks 11–12
Milestone Celebration + Sustained Cadence
Celebrate any membership milestones publicly ("50 Hawaii Island businesses now on HIBCW — mahalo!"). Post a 12-week campaign wrap summary on LinkedIn. Set up a monthly content cadence: one Nextdoor district update, two Instagram posts, one LinkedIn piece, and one email per month to sustain awareness after the launch push.

Measurement

KPIs & Tracking

What gets measured gets managed. These are the metrics that actually tell you whether the campaign is working — not vanity numbers like follower count.

Primary metric: Registered businesses on HIBCW. Everything else — social reach, video views, email opens — is a leading indicator. The number that matters most is how many Hawaii Island businesses are actively participating in the network.
Phase 1 Goals · Weeks 1–4

KIAA Onboarding

  • 20+ KIAA members registered on HIBCW
  • Email open rate ≥ 35%
  • Demo session: 15+ attendees
  • Active incidents from 3+ districts
  • 100+ website visits from email links
Phase 2 Goals · Weeks 5–12

Island-Wide Growth

  • 50+ total registered businesses
  • All 9 districts with at least 1 member
  • Instagram: 200+ followers gained
  • YouTube explainer: 500+ views
  • Nextdoor posts: 3 district engagements
Ongoing · Month 3+

Sustained Health

  • 5+ new businesses per month
  • Monthly active incident reports
  • Email list growth from referrals
  • At least 1 press mention secured
  • District captain retention: all 9 active
Tracking Setup

What to Measure & How

Website Traffic

Google Analytics (Free)

Add Google Analytics 4 to hibcw.vercel.app to track page views, session source (email vs Instagram vs Nextdoor vs direct), and which pages visitors land on. This tells you which channels are driving actual traffic vs. just impressions.

Link Tracking

UTM Parameters

Add ?utm_source=instagram, ?utm_source=nextdoor, etc. to every URL you post on social media. Then Analytics shows you exactly which post drove which visits. Free. Takes 30 seconds per link.

Email

Open Rate + Click Rate

Track via Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or whatever email platform KIAA uses. Goal: 35%+ open rate (achievable with a warm list like KIAA members). Click rate goal: 10%+ on launch email.

Social

Reach & Saves (not Likes)

On Instagram, track Reach and Saves — not Likes. Saves indicate the content was useful enough to return to. On LinkedIn, track impressions and profile visits. On Nextdoor, track replies and direct messages from business owners.

Monthly Reporting Cadence: At the end of each month, compile a simple one-page report: total registered businesses, new registrations this month, top-performing post, website sessions by source, and one qualitative win (member story, press mention, captain note). Share with KIAA leadership to maintain organizational buy-in for the program.
