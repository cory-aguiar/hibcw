How to Report an Incident
When you see something suspicious or criminal, your report to HIBCW helps protect every business in your district. Here's exactly what to do — step by step.
🔴
Call 911 First if Any of These Apply
⚡ A crime is actively in progress or someone is in immediate danger
⚡ There is a weapon involved or a physical threat to any person
⚡ Someone is injured or needs medical attention
⚡ A suspect is still on the premises or fleeing the area right now
Step-by-Step: Reporting to HIBCW
1
Stay Safe & Observe
Do not confront a suspect. From a safe position, take note of everything you can: time, location, description of persons, vehicles, and actions. If it's safe to do so, take photos or video from a distance.
📸 Photo or video evidence is extremely helpful
2
Go to hibcw.vercel.app
Open your phone or computer browser and visit hibcw.vercel.app. Click "Report an Incident" on the home page. No account or login is required — anyone can submit a report.
📱 Works on any smartphone browser
3
Fill In the Incident Details
Complete as many fields as you can. The most important are: type of incident, exact address or cross-streets, time it occurred, and a brief description. Don't worry if you don't have every detail — partial information is still valuable.
📍 Pin the location on the map for accuracy
4
Describe the Suspect & Vehicle (if applicable)
Use the fields below as a guide. Include as much as you can remember. Even partial descriptions — a shirt color, vehicle make, or approximate age — can help law enforcement and your District Captain identify patterns.
5
Submit & Watch for District Alerts
Click Submit. Your report instantly appears on the live HIBCW map. Your District Captain will review it and may issue a district-wide alert if the threat is ongoing. Keep an eye on hibcw.vercel.app for updates in your area.
🔔 Share the site with your staff so they see alerts too
What to Note — Description Guide
👤 Suspect Description
Gender
Male / Female / Unknown
Age (est.)
Teens / 20s / 30s / 40s / 50s+
Height (est.)
Short / Medium / Tall & approx. feet
Build
Slim / Medium / Heavy / Athletic
Clothing
Shirt color, pants, shoes, hat
Hair
Color, length, style
Other
Tattoos, facial hair, glasses, accent
Direction
Which way they left & on foot or vehicle
🚗 Vehicle Description
Make
Toyota, Ford, Honda, etc.
Model
Sedan, Pickup, SUV, Van, etc.
Color
Primary & any secondary color
Year (est.)
Approximate decade is fine
License Plate
Full plate or even partial letters/numbers
Condition
Dents, damage, decals, tinted windows
Direction
Which direction vehicle traveled
# Occupants
How many people were in the vehicle
