How to Report an Incident
When you see something suspicious or criminal, your report helps protect every business in your district. Here's exactly what to do.
🔴
Call 911 First if Any of These Apply
⚡ Crime actively in progress or immediate danger
⚡ Weapon involved or physical threat to a person
⚡ Someone is injured or needs medical attention
⚡ Suspect still on premises or fleeing right now
Step-by-Step: Reporting to HIBCW
1
Stay Safe & Observe
Do not confront a suspect. Note: time, location, descriptions of persons, vehicles, and actions. Take photos or video from a safe distance if possible. 📸 Photos & video are extremely helpful
2
Go to hibcw.vercel.app
Open any browser and visit hibcw.vercel.app . Click "Report an Incident." No account or login required — anyone can submit. 📱 Works on any smartphone
3
Fill In the Incident Details
Complete as many fields as possible: type of incident, address or cross-streets, time, and description. Partial information is still valuable. 📍 Pin the location on the map for accuracy
4
Describe the Suspect & Vehicle
Use the guide below. Even partial descriptions — a shirt color, vehicle make, or approximate age — help law enforcement and your District Captain identify patterns.
5
Submit & Watch for District Alerts
Click Submit — your report instantly appears on the live HIBCW map. Your District Captain may issue a district-wide alert if the threat is ongoing. 🔔 Share the site with staff so they see alerts too
What to Note — Description Guide
👤 Suspect Description
Gender
Male / Female / Unknown
Age (est.)
Teens / 20s / 30s / 40s / 50s+
Height (est.)
Short / Medium / Tall & approx. ft
Build
Slim / Medium / Heavy / Athletic
Clothing
Shirt color, pants, shoes, hat
Hair
Color, length, style
Other
Tattoos, facial hair, glasses, accent
Direction
Which way & on foot or vehicle
🚗 Vehicle Description
Make
Toyota, Ford, Honda, etc.
Model
Sedan, Pickup, SUV, Van, etc.
Color
Primary & any secondary color
Year (est.)
Approximate decade is fine
License Plate
Full or partial letters/numbers
Condition
Dents, damage, decals, tint
Direction
Which direction vehicle traveled
# Occupants
How many people in the vehicle
