01 · Incident Type
Incident Type Glossary
Use these definitions to select the most accurate incident type. When in doubt, choose Suspicious Activity or Other and describe the situation in detail.
🔴 Serious Incidents
🔫
Robbery
Taking property directly from a person by force, threat, or intimidation — e.g. a mugging, armed hold-up, or carjacking. The victim is present during the crime. Always call 911 if in progress.
🏠
Burglary
Unlawful entry into a building, home, or structure to commit a crime inside — typically theft. The victim does not need to be present. Includes break-ins to sheds, garages, and storage units.
🤜
Assault
Physical attack or credible threat of bodily harm against a person — includes hitting, fighting, brandishing a weapon, or threatening language accompanied by aggressive behavior.
💊
Drug Activity
Visible or suspected drug use, sales, or distribution in a public or community space — e.g. open use in a parking lot, repeated foot-traffic consistent with dealing, or drug paraphernalia left in a shared area.
🟡 Moderate Incidents
🛍
Theft / Shoplifting
Taking property without force and without confronting the victim — includes shoplifting from a store, stealing items left unattended, or theft from an unlocked vehicle or yard.
🔨
Vandalism
Intentional destruction or defacement of property — broken windows, slashed tires, damaged fencing, graffiti tags on private or commercial property, or destruction of signage.
🚗
Vehicle Break-In
Forced or unauthorized entry into a parked vehicle — smashed windows, pried doors, or evidence of someone rummaging through a car, truck, or other vehicle. Use Robbery if the owner was present and confronted.
💳
Fraud / Scam
Deceptive schemes intended to obtain money, goods, or personal information — includes phone or email scams targeting community members, skimming devices on ATMs or gas pumps, identity theft, or contractor fraud.
🔵 Observation & Other
🚫
Trespassing
Someone entering or remaining on private property without permission — including posted "No Trespassing" areas, fenced lots, closed business premises after hours, or repeatedly accessing a neighbor's property uninvited.
👁
Suspicious Activity
Behavior that seems out of place or concerning but doesn't clearly fit another category — e.g. someone casing businesses, unfamiliar vehicles parked for long periods, individuals looking into cars or peering into homes.
🎨
Graffiti
Unsanctioned writing, tags, or artwork on public or private surfaces — walls, utility boxes, road signs, restrooms, or business exteriors. Use Vandalism if other property damage accompanied it.
📋
Other
An incident that doesn't fit any of the above categories. Please use the description field (section 04) to explain what occurred in as much detail as possible so it can be properly reviewed.
